Step 5

Flash The Sensor Pico

Waiting
  1. Unplug the Sensor Pico.
  2. Hold BOOTSEL while plugging it into this computer.
  3. Wait for the installer to detect the BOOTSEL drive.
  4. Click Use Detected Pico For Sensor Node. The installer will flash either a Pico W or Pico 2 W (RP2350) with the matching sensor firmware, provision Wi-Fi and MQTT, then wait for the node to appear and assign it automatically.
  5. After flashing finishes, unplug the Pico from this computer and reconnect it to the actual sensor hardware so it can read the probe and report to the Pi.

Step 6

Flash The Actuator Pico

Waiting
  1. Unplug the Sensor Pico after it reboots.
  2. Hold BOOTSEL while plugging in the Actuator Pico.
  3. Wait for the installer to detect the BOOTSEL drive.
  4. Click Use Detected Pico For Actuator Node. The installer will flash either a Pico W or Pico 2 W (RP2350) with the matching actuator firmware and provision the saved Wi-Fi and MQTT settings.
  5. After provisioning finishes, unplug the Pico from this computer and reconnect it to the actual actuator hardware so it can receive watering commands from the Pi.
  6. The installer will wait until the actuator node appears on the Pi before it marks this step complete.

Step 8

Calibrate The Sensor Node

Waiting

Prepare two containers of soil before you continue. One container should have dry soil. The other should have saturated soil. Place the sensor probe in the dry soil first, then capture ten readings for the dry calibration. After that, move the sensor into the saturated soil and capture ten readings for the wet calibration. You can recalibrate this sensor later anytime in the Victory Garden app.
